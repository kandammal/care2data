DRUG NAME: Metformin
DRUG CLASS: Biguanide

MECHANISM OF ACTION:
- Primary: Inhibits mitochondrial respiratory chain complex I, reducing hepatic gluconeogenesis
- Gi Effects: Increases intestinal serotonin secretion and alters gut microbiome
- Lactic Acidosis: Inhibits lactate clearance in liver and kidneys, increases lactate production in intestines

COMMON ADVERSE EFFECTS:
- nausea
- diarrhea
- abdominal pain
- flatulence
- vitamin B12 deficiency
- metallic taste

SERIOUS ADVERSE EFFECTS:
- lactic acidosis
- hypoglycemia
- renal failure

RISK FACTORS:
- renal impairment (eGFR <45)
- elderly (>65 years)
- hepatic dysfunction
- dehydration
- contrast agents
- alcohol abuse
- heart failure

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- severe renal impairment
- metabolic acidosis
- acute heart failure
