DRUG NAME: Lisinopril
DRUG CLASS: ACE Inhibitor

MECHANISM OF ACTION:
- Primary: Inhibits angiotensin-converting enzyme, reducing angiotensin II formation
- Cough: Accumulation of bradykinin and substance P in respiratory tract
- Renal Effects: Dilates efferent arteriole reducing intraglomerular pressure

COMMON ADVERSE EFFECTS:
- cough
- hypotension
- dizziness
- headache
- fatigue
- nausea
- hyperkalemia

SERIOUS ADVERSE EFFECTS:
- angioedema
- acute renal failure
- hyperkalemia
- hepatotoxicity
- neutropenia

RISK FACTORS:
- renal artery stenosis
- volume depletion
- elderly
- concurrent NSAIDs
- potassium supplements
