DRUG NAME: Sertraline
DRUG CLASS: Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitor (SSRI)

MECHANISM OF ACTION:
- Primary: Selectively inhibits presynaptic serotonin reuptake, increasing synaptic 5-HT
- Gi Effects: Activates 5-HT3 and 5-HT4 receptors in GI tract causing nausea
- Cns Effects: Modulates multiple 5-HT receptor subtypes affecting mood, sleep, anxiety

COMMON ADVERSE EFFECTS:
- nausea
- diarrhea
- insomnia
- somnolence
- dizziness
- tremor
- sexual dysfunction
- dry mouth
- increased sweating

SERIOUS ADVERSE EFFECTS:
- serotonin syndrome
- suicidal ideation
- bleeding
- hyponatremia
- seizures
- QT prolongation

RISK FACTORS:
- concurrent MAOIs
- bleeding disorders
- seizure history
- bipolar disorder
- hyponatremia risk
- elderly
