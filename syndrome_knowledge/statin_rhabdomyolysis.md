SYNDROME: Statin-Induced Rhabdomyolysis

DRUGS:
- Atorvastatin
- Simvastatin
- Other Statins

KEY SYMPTOMS:
- Severe muscle pain
- Muscle weakness
- Dark urine (myoglobinuria)

RISK FACTORS:
- Elderly
- Renal impairment
- Hypothyroidism
- Drug interactions (macrolides, azoles, fibrates)

PATHOPHYSIOLOGY:
Statins deplete CoQ10 and impair mitochondrial ATP synthesis causing muscle breakdown and myoglobin release.

DIAGNOSTIC MARKERS:
- CK > 10x ULN
- Myoglobinuria
- Elevated creatinine

CLINICAL ACTION:
- Stop statin immediately
- IV fluids
- Monitor CK, renal function

SEVERITY:
High to Life-threatening

COMPLICATIONS:
- Acute kidney injury
- DIC
- Compartment syndrome

FDA, UpToDate, PubMed
